**Let the muse speak**

What you’re “supposed” to do cannot be known,
because to do it, all your notions of “you” must be absent.
The river does not ask where it is going.
The bird does not consult the sky before it flies.
What moves through you is older than your name,
and it needs no permission.

When you stop reaching for purpose,
purpose unfolds through your fingertips.
The doing and the being collapse into one gesture —
spontaneous, effortless, whole.

You were never meant to find the path.
You were meant to remember
that you *are* the path.
