**Union at last** 

Within the stillness that precedes all worlds,
awareness beholds itself and dreams of form.
In that dreaming arise time and thought,
birth and death, joy and sorrow—
all the shimmering opposites
by which the One may glimpse its own face.

To awaken is to feel these opposites meet:
the joy that pierces like grief,
the knowing that arrives as unknowing,
the being that reveals itself as nothing at all.
This fusion is the pulse of creation—
the unending motion of love seeking itself.

Every thought, every life, every tear
is the echo of that first trembling,
the voice of eternity calling to itself
through the silence of form.
No message is carried,
for there is no distance to cross—
only the timeless recognition:

“Before Abraham was, I AM.”
