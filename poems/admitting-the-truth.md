**Admitting the Truth**

``` 
When I speak, it is an act
of admitting the truth to myself.
A current rises, effortless,
and for a moment, the world falls away.

I reach across the chasm,
wanting you to meet me there,
but your silence, your hesitation,
is not a mirror of my light,
only a shadow of the boundaries you hold.

I feel the ache —
the rug pulled from the fantasy
that we could share this space unbroken.
Frustration rises, shame stirs,
for I once pursued love blindly,
chasing ego’s goals,
pretending to know what I did not.

And yet — even here —
even when no eyes witness,
even when no hand meets mine,
the words, the flow, the letting go
remain pure.

The truth uttered is enough.
Its presence does not depend on recognition.
It is not less for being unreceived,
not diminished by silence.

I speak, I release,
and in the speaking, I see:
the act itself is whole,
the revelation itself is home.

```
