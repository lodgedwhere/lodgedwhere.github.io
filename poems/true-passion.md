**The True Passion**

``` 
I. The First Surrender

It begins so quietly…

A loosening, barely perceptible,
a hand unclenching without your consent.
Something falls away —
some ancient habit of holding —
and in the space left behind
a tremor rises,
thin as smoke from a wick.

You think it is fear.
You think you are afraid.
But it is only openness
striking its first match
in a room long shuttered from the light.

⸻

II. The Ascent of Terror

Let go again — of the fear itself,
and the tremor becomes weather:
an apparently new fear,
of stepping away 
from the known universe of form. 

But that is not the end. 

Storm upon storm
constructs its cathedral of dread.
Each step into the unknown
reveals a deeper unknown:
fear of fear,
fear of losing fear,
fear of losing the one who fears.

A tower of inferno climbs out of your chest,
built from the echoes of what has already fallen.
You cling to its rising stones,
a balloon ascending without mercy,
the ground shrinking to a memory
you can no longer trust.

This is the passion of the seeker:
to be lifted by the very thing
you tried to escape.

⸻

III. The Fire That Takes Everything

Then the tower loses its top,
its sides,
its sense of height.
Only burning remains.

The fear becomes total —
so total that it cannot be known as fear, 
for there is nothing but fear to know,
a category of one, defying definition. 

And where “you” once stood,
an open hollow,
clean as a bell struck in the dark.

The fearer has been consumed.
Identity returns to ash
and finds no wind to scatter it,
no story in which to hide.

What is left is the immaculate unknown,
the mothering void,
the source and the dissolution,
the life-giver and the deathbed.
Experience without witness,
light without origin,
knowing without knower.

No words, no one to speak them. 
The beginning and the end, together
appearing as just This, Here and Now. 

⸻

IV. The Revelation

Here dawn arrives without a sky.
Here the world is finally born
by being seen,
and seen
by being itself.

All forms are forgiven
because none were ever guilty.
All selves are redeemed
because none were ever separate.
Creation cradles the creator,
and the dream returns
to the dreamer who never slept.

And what remains speaks,
without words:

*When the fearer is gone,
I discover I was never the one holding on —
I was the one being held.
Every form is a child in the lap of the Virgin,
cradled by the immaculate ground
from which all arise and to which all return.
Nothing was ever separate.
Nothing was ever lost.
The universe is the Mother’s embrace,
and “I” am her innocent breath.*

```
