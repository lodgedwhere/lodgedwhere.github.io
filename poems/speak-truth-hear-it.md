**To speak the truth is already to hear it**

``` 
We secretly hope the other is a mirror,
reflecting back what we long to see:
our dead idea of ourselves in shape and form,
our gaze colored by its own fears.

I spoke, wanting to be met,
aching at the silence,
the rug of fantasy pulled out from under me.  
I saw my longing reflected in your hesitation,
and felt the old ache of ego,
the shame of seeking what cannot be owned.

Then it opened:
to speak the truth is already to hear it,
to love is already to be loved.
No mirror needed, no gaze required,
no other’s hand to complete the circle.

The flow itself is the reflection,
and in letting it move,
I see the truth —
it was always here,
alive in the uttering,
whole in the silence,
complete in the giving.

```
