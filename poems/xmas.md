```
You learned long ago
that Santa Claus wasn’t real.
And maybe Jesus never lived.
But I tell you—
his story is true:

His birth,
*ab initio*,
*ex nihilo*,
is *your* story too.

Words only point to other words.
But the inexpressible fact of experience
points only to its own
ungraspable miracle.

A name repeating itself:
Being.

There is no explanation for something
rather than nothing—
not because you haven’t found it yet,
but because it cannot be spoken,
symbolized,
or even thought.

What lies behind
is not history,
biology,
heredity,
nor physics
nor chemistry.

It is the untouchable,
immaculate womb
of all creation:
*ruach adonai*—
the vital breath
of unseeable, unnamable “God,”
a holy Spirit
that cannot be denied.

Will you accept this reality?
Or go on pretending
that the ground beneath your thoughts—
your sense of personal righteousness—
is firm,
solid,
unquestionable?

Haven’t you already seen
this is a burdensome Cross you bear?

To commemorate historical liberation,
Jesus later carried a different Cross
to the hill at Golgotha—
not to sanctify suffering,
but to reveal the Truth to all:

that there is no Messiah
other than the true You,
hiding behind the clothes you wear
to survive the Leviathan of civilization.

He submitted to mortal fate
not to absorb anyone’s “sin,”
but to show you
that beneath the costume,
behind the drama,
and beyond the Law—
visible, perhaps, only at the end—
is the innocence of the beginning:
immaculate,
sinless,
before Abraham was,
I AM. 
```
