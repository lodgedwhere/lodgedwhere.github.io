**Whereof...**

(To L.W., RIP)

``` 
In black and white,
you showed that language is inert.

Each line the beginning 
of a book you would never write.

With your devastating “Whereof…”,
you made fools of us all.

The rational mind sees through every word:
the building blocks of prepositions 
can never construct more than tautology.

And yet I persist in using them.
You answered this paradox too:
as a thorn removes another,
words undo our bondage
to the “meaning” of others.
Then both can be discarded.

We have professors of philosophy,
but few true philosophers.
You gave us the good and bad news both:
that philosophy, though self-negating,
must be preserved
as long as we remain deluded.
```
