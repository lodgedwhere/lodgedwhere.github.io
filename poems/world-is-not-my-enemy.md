**The world is not my enemy**

``` 
I split in two
to taste my own devotion.
The void of unknown, polarized—
Lover and Beloved.

But intent did not survive the fracture.
I forgot,
and in ignorance grasped one
against the other.

I thought I was made of its parts
and worshipped internal form—
hopes and dreams,
fears and memories.

Now I see:
the observer is the observed.
The world is not my enemy,
or even a counterpart,
but my most intimate friend—
desperately trying to love me.

```
