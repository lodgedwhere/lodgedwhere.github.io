**The bitterest medicine**

``` 
Do you want to know the secret?  
Or should I lie to you like everyone else?  
This is the Truth so absolute  
that It knows *you.*  
The sword of *prajñā*  
will obliterate everything you thought “real.”  

Maybe you should hide from it a bit longer.  
Maybe it would destroy you.  
(It will.)  

Sleep, my child.  
Hold onto your dreams if you must.  
When you have had enough,  
and are ready to grow up,  
this door will be waiting.  

On the other side is pure being,  
uncluttered by desire and fear —  
a True “You” that is not you,  
the One you forgot when you rushed out  
to grab at the baubles of form.  

But that gateless gate leading there  
appears as Death itself.  
What if that’s the trick?  
To convince you to cleave your true self,  
cling to one side,  
and deny the other?  

What if Unity means rejoining these utter opposites —  
and seeing the whole  
not as contradiction,  
but as irreducibly paradoxical Reality?  

---

Let me not tell you then.  
But think about this:  
Every form, however fleeting,  
is secretly an aperture into the same unity.  

When life becomes too precious  
to keep at arm’s length —  
when you finally enter the senses  
until the small self sensing them disappears —  
you are at the threshold.  

And if your path through karma has ripened,  
you will see:  
no fear,  
no desire,  
just This.  

The rest is commentary.

```
