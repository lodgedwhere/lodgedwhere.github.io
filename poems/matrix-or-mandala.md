**Matrix or mandala?**

``` 
At first it appears as a pattern —
a grid of relations,
a lattice of distinctions,
drawn to make sense of the whole.
Each line crosses another,
and meaning flickers at the intersections.

I trace the pattern inward,
naming the points,
mapping the distances.
It feels like knowledge.
But the more complete the map becomes,
the less there is to hold.

Somewhere the lines begin to bend,
returning toward themselves.
The grid folds inward like a mandala,
each symmetry revealing not the world,
but the act of drawing itself.

In the center — no center.
Only the still eye of awareness,
watching its own geometry dissolve.
Every square, every gate, every mirrored face
was a doorway into the same silence.

The matrix was never a structure to understand,
but a ritual to exhaust understanding —
a mandala traced in thought
so that thought could fall away.

And when it does,
the design remains,
weightless and whole,
like breath on a mirror
that never needed reflection.

```
