**Language to manifest a mirror**

The only thing worth symbolizing is the totality of your own experience—to explain yourself to yourself. Otherwise you wander in confusion, forever seeking “meaning” in your doing: the eye cannot see itself; the knife cannot cut itself.

In spoken language, this pursuit inevitably takes a poetic form, for speech was made for communication, not for revelation. It was never meant to turn inward toward the source. Mathematics, by contrast, was forged for precision—to describe phenomena clearly. Yet it too can become a mirror, reflecting the whole of your experience, if the world is seen as it truly is.
