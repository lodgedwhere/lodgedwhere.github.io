**A thought-march to emptiness**

*A concise synthesis of all conclusions that follow from the Tractatus*

	1.	Language can only refer to present facts.

In the Tractatus, Wittgenstein holds that a proposition pictures only what is the case now.

A “past” fact or “future” fact is never given, and therefore cannot be meaningfully spoken of.

	2.	Thought cannot exceed language.

If all thought is symbolic—inner speech, conceptual form, imagery—then thought is bound by the limits of meaningful language.

What cannot be said cannot, in the strict sense, be thought.

	3.	Past and future cannot be thought.

What we call “past” is simply present memory-imagery.
What we call “future” is present anticipation-simulation.
Neither exists as independent realities.
Therefore: continuous time is a mental construct, not an experienced dimension.
There is only Now.

	4.	Elsewhere cannot be thought.

Just as you never access a past or future, you never access an actual “over there.”
Every spatial reference is a present image or concept.
There is only Here.

	5.	Ego depends entirely on past and future, here and elsewhere.

The ego’s narrative comes from memory (nonexistent past),
and its desires, fears, and plans come from prediction (nonexistent future).
Its sense of location and separateness comes from imagined elsewhere.
Remove these, and the ego has no substance.
Ego is a hallucination of time and space.

	6.	Meaning collapses with the ego.

Meaning always implies a subject who holds meaning.
When no enduring “you” can be found in the only real domain (the present),
meaning loses its foothold.
Form becomes a hall of mirrors — appearances reflecting appearances with no referent.

	7.	Search for the self reveals its absence.

When attention looks for “me” in the present (the only real place),
nothing is found.
The observer cannot be made into an object:
the knife cannot cut itself, the eye cannot see itself.
The self does not disappear; it was never present.

	8.	Clarity reveals emptiness.

What remains when illusion dissipates is not some positive new state,
but the unobstructed recognition that all forms lack inherent essence.
Clarity does not replace emptiness;
clarity unveils emptiness.

⸻

In one sentence:

Thought, bound by the Tractarian limits of language, can find only Here–Now;
and in the Here–Now, no self, no time, no place, and no meaning exist —
only emptiness revealed in perfect clarity.
