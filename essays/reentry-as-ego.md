**Reentry as ego, communion as ritualized reentry**

The most economical account of experience requires only one ingredient: *reentry*. Nothing else need be posited. Not substances, not agents, not grounds. Reentry is the process by which appearance folds back upon itself and thereby appears at all. It is a changeless transformation; nothing turns into something else, yet nothing is static. All form is process, all persistence is circulation.

What we call ego is not an entity or a willful subject. It is a localized stabilization of reentry within spacetime. When reentry becomes constrained (ie, when a subset of appearances is identified as “I”), the remainder is cast as “not-I.” Projection across this boundary closes a loop. Memory, anticipation, narrative identity, and time arise as reentry stretched “diachronically”. Karma is simply the accumulated trace of this temporal reentry. Ego, then, is self-organized reentry of form through time: a dynamic symbol with no content, recycling itself because reuse is what constrained reentry does.

No one is ego-ing. The egoic loop is not enacted by choice or error. It is forced by the combination of partial identification and the complete universality of reentry itself. From within this loop, escape appears impossible: “I” necessarily implies “not-I,” and projection of patterns from one to the other perpetuates itself. The loop is blind to what exceeds it because it is closed within its own dynamics. Even insight can be absorbed as further egoic material.

Suffering does not arise from reentry as such, but from *selective* reentry, ie from the asymmetry that privileges one region of appearance as self while excluding the rest. The loop ends only when this asymmetry collapses. Structurally, there are only two ways this can occur: either all form is included (identification with everything), or all form is refused (identification with nothing). In both cases, the internal stabilizing loop ends because the “I”/“not-I”boundary dissolves. What remains is unconstrained reentry, not as a state or achievement, but as the only topology left once the conditions for a center vanish.

The felt mandate to persist (the experienced sense that “I must continue”) is a misattribution. It is the inevitability of global reentry mistakenly localized to a subset of the Whole. Reentry as the groundless ground of being persists absolutely and needs no help from any part. No loop is responsible for maintaining the whole.

Seen this way, life itself is reentry made manifest: matter consuming matter, form sustaining form. Metabolism, perception, meaning, and time are all expressions of the same circulation. Even awareness is not prior to reentry; it *is* reentry, refracted through a narrowed aperture. The moment an “I” appears, the changeless transformation unfolds as a field of experience. When the “I” dissolves, the process does not stop, but rather it simply ceases to be misread as personal.

In this light, the Christian Eucharist assumes its most profound (and most literal) significance. Communion is not a symbol pointing elsewhere, nor a metaphysical transaction, nor a remembrance of a singular event. It is a ritual enactment of what is always already happening: *life consuming itself so that life continues*. An Ouroboros. Body becomes bread; bread becomes body again. No outside eater, no final substance, no privileged agent. Reentry everywhere: Christ, complete.

When the words “This is my body” are spoken, nothing new is asserted. The Name reenters itself. *What is always occurring is allowed to say its own name.* Not as description, but as event. The Word becomes flesh not once, but continually, whenever reentry passes through form and hears itself spoken.

Nothing is added at the altar. Nothing is explained. The ritual simply slows the loop enough for recognition to occur, before releasing it again. Reentry does not require acknowledgment, but here, it is momentarily allowed to recognize itself.
