**Personal analogy**

As Spencer-Brown was to the original insight,
I hope to be to its interpretation —
in the same way Paul gave language to what Jesus revealed. 

What strikes me most about the analogy is that Paul was not simply a messenger.
He was a man broken open.

First, he was not Paul but *Saul*. 
Saul was the hyper-rational enforcer of orthodoxy—the persecutor of heresy, the embodiment of certainty and identity. He was the one who believed the law was absolute, the world was fixed, and the self was stable.

Paul was what remained after the shattering: a man who saw the truth directly, without mediation, and could no longer pretend that the old worldview meant anything.

The transformation wasn’t cosmetic.
It was existential.

I see my own path in this.

I was once embedded in the hyper-formal edifice of physics. I pursued it with Saul-like intensity, enforcing its norms on myself, believing that the map was the territory and that meaning could be extracted by force of intellect alone. I lived inside the distinction I/not-I as if it were real and absolute.

Then came the Awakening— the void’s expression as Ouroboros, the collapse of observer and observed.
The rational edifice fractured.
My sense of self annihilated.
I died into a new way of knowing.

What remained afterward was not Saul.

It was Paul:
the part of me that sees the structure behind the structure,
that understands how the map and the hand that draws it are the same movement,
that feels the tautology of Being from within, rather than analyzing it from without.

I now walk between worlds—mathematics and metaphysics—with a bridge already built inside me. My transformation was not a rejection of the old system, but its completion. My awakening did not destroy mathematics; it revealed its root. My matrix does not contradict physics; it explains its blind spot. My reading of Spencer-Brown does not replace him; it fulfills him.

The symbolism is clear to me now:

I was Saul.
I became Paul.
And the second act of Spencer-Brown’s work is the natural consequence of that rebirth.
