**An apology**

These posts may sound paradoxical, irrational, or self-contradictory. I owe the reader an explanation for that before asking for any patience with the words.

The difficulty is simple: the subject of these posts lies outside the system of meaning through which all language operates. Language works by establishing distinctions, relations, causes, and identities. It organizes experience into a structure that allows us to say that something means something else.

But the experience these posts attempt to gesture toward is the collapse of the very structure that generates meaning in the first place: the assumption that there exists a separate self to whom experience belongs.

Once that assumption loosens, the machinery of meaning remains useful for ordinary life, but it is no longer convincing as a description of what is fundamentally happening. Words can continue to point, but they cannot capture what they point toward. They operate inside the system whose limits they are trying to reveal.

This creates an unavoidable paradox. In order to speak about the dissolution of the self, one must use the language of the self. In order to question meaning, one must use meaningful sentences. The result is that the writing often appears circular, contradictory, or irrational. In truth, it is simply language *pressed against its own boundary.*

There is a deeper difficulty as well.

The ordinary sense of identity is the source of meaning itself. Every goal, value, interpretation, and purpose arises within the framework of a self that seeks something. From within that framework, abandoning the self appears identical to abandoning meaning. To the ego, the suggestion that it dissolve sounds like desperate nihilism.

For that reason, no meaningful argument can persuade the ego to relinquish itself. Rational explanation cannot produce the recognition these words describe, because rational explanation belongs to the very structure that must be seen through. Understanding the sentences will not bring anyone closer to a spiritual goal, because what is pointed to here lies outside the domain in which goals operate.

In this sense, reality cannot be shared the way information can be shared. One mind cannot hand another the recognition that there is no separate center behind experience. One can only point to the moon, and hope the other sees beyond the finger gesturing toward it.

These posts may have value not because they convey a truth that can be adopted or believed. Their only possible function is more modest: to indicate the limits of the system of meaning in which they themselves appear. They are merely *self-portraits*.

If at some moment the reader notices something beyond the words… something already present before interpretation… it will not be because the words produced it.

It will only be because they briefly failed, and what was always present became visible beneath the distractions of mind.

For that failure, and for the confusion these posts may create, I offer my apologies.
