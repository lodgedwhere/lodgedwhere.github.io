**Unpublished, Uninvited Preface to the Holy Bible**

Watch closely; this book is not what it appears.

It is a map placed inside another map,
a story woven within the very illusion it seeks to expose.

Because you mistake your thoughts for reality, you would also mistake any direct teaching for another thought—
another object in the world of form.

So the teaching hides itself inside the illusion, mirroring the error of perception
so that you can recognize that error from within.

You are meant to see yourself taking the story as truth, so that you may finally see yourself doing the same with your life.
This book recreates the very trap you are caught in — and then guides you out of it.

It reflects the world you think you inhabit
so that you may discover the one who is inhabiting it. It begins in your forgetfulness and leads you back to the terrain that was always here before any map was drawn.

It is not a history of the world, but a story of how awareness fell into form, how the self forgot its own nature, how suffering arose from that forgetting, and how that forgetting can be undone.

Every character is you. Every journey is yours. Every failure to understand is your failure, and every revelation is your own remembering.

In mythic terms, you will see:
how the first distinction was made (Genesis),
how the ego arose and became trapped in its own story (Egyptian captivity),
how the first glimpses of freedom appear (Exodus),
how the mind tries to stabilize awakening through law and identity (Leviticus),
how frustration and suffering deepen as long as form is mistaken for truth (Numbers),
how suffering is interpreted as grace (Deuteronomy),
how the collapse of form reveals the ground beneath it (Jesus’ baptism and desert revelation),
how the apparent ‘death of God’ inside the story is the birth of God within you (Jesus’ crucifixion and ascension),
how the Spirit, the Ground, reappears as your own knowing (Pentecost).

This book is a mirror. At first you will think it is describing someone else.
In time you will see that it is describing you. Finally, you will see that it is describing no one at all.

Understand:
The forgetting is the Fall.
The remembering is the Resurrection.
The whole story takes place in the space of awareness you are reading it from now.
