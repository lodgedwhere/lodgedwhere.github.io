**Autopsy of fear**

I was once so thoroughly afraid of death that I buried it as deep as it would go and hid in an identity I knew could not protect me from it. Eventually I had to confront the reality of death, to figure it out somehow, to find a way to convince myself that my doing still mattered in its shadow. 

But when I opened up to try to understand, I really saw, knew in my being, that existence and mortality are inextricably linked — that they are exactly the same, with no way out.  Every moment is suffused with death, making it the most important problem to solve: without its solution, no other problem was worth solving, and life itself had no meaning.

But its identity with life was absolutely, totally unbreakable. And when I finally allowed myself to see that, to admit it to myself, I was suddenly “in” on the cruel joke of existence — and I wanted out.

I used everything I could to withdraw from my senses, to escape the unbearable fact of being. But seeing that I could not do this indefinitely, I plunged back in — into, and through the senses. I buried “myself” in them until I became them - the entire field of sensing, all as One - letting go of everything and accepting annihilation. 

As if the only way to hide in Hell is in Satan’s furnace.

But what was revealed in this state of no-self is no horror. It is simply a way of true being, outside the realm of what can be thought about: no-mind. Death is accepted so unconditionally that you go beyond it — into a world untouched by the polarization of duality. This epic absence, the void of nonexistence, is in reality infinite fullness, completion, the home you never left. 

Only you get no souvenirs. There’s no way to think about what cannot be conceptualized by the mind. It cannot be understood, but also cannot be discarded. It is more absolutely true than anything called “real”. 

But this ineffability is what gives such an experience transcendence. The body of fear lies open — every organ named, every illusion stilled —
and yet what remains cannot die.

Isn’t the trick you *can’t* explain the most magical?
