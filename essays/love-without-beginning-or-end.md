**A love without beginning or end**

To love completely is to give yourself over completely: to surrender even understanding and meaning, leaving the present moment bare, and utterly indescribable.

When that cipher of meaning — the ego —falls away, nothing remains to surrender and no one to do the surrendering.

And yet the Loving remains: in duality, as lover united with beloved, but in absolute truth as itself, without subject or object.

This pure Love is the unmarked state, the medium from which the ten thousand things arise by distinction alone. Its bare, uncompletable nature (Love without lover or beloved) is precisely the beginning and the end, Now: the alpha and omega of the present moment.

Because it cannot be completed, it cannot end; because it never began, it is always already here.

The one who knows, speaks, or types — the “doer”, “me” — appears only after this absolute unity is fractured by distinction, and thus cannot be the true “I”, the imagined Experiencer.

What unexamined action flows from this pure unrestricted state is not right or wrong, but Love itself, needing no ground: Love loving itself.
