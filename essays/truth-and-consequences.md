**Truth and Consequences**

Jesus taught me that to speak the truth with love, you must first be completely willing to see that you live in a time and place where survival is believed to depend on the contradiction of that truth.

He walked into the fire and spoke anyway, because it had to be said *then* in order to be heard *now*.

He sacrificed himself by submitting to the judgment of those who could not yet see the cruel absurdity of asking, “What is truth?” in the presence of what they refused to recognize.

This truth had to pass through condemnation so that it could later be recognized from outside the machinery that killed its messenger. To hear it now, you must already see that Pilate’s ignorance is not only still present, but *amplified* in the environment that continues to condition you and everyone you know.

And in sacrifice, Jesus did more than speak the truth. He gave the only instruction worth giving to fellow mortal man: how to die without suffering death— by seeing that no one ever left the cradling womb of the Virgin, the immaculate, perfect formlessness from which all form arises and into which it dissolves.

That is why the cross is not payment, but exposure: impossible to deny, to rationalize, or to refuse. He did not die to atone for your sins, but to show you that your sins are already forgiven, once you admit the truth you already know — to yourself! — whatever the consequences.
