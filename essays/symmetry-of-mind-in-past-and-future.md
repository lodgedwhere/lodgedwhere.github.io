**A symmetry of mind in past and future**

Notice a symmetry in how the mind relates to experience across time: *Desire* and *fear* are directed toward the future: desire reaches forward, trying to grasp what is not yet here, while fear anticipates and pushes away what might come. In parallel, *highlights* and *trauma* are oriented toward the past: highlights cling to what was interpreted as pleasant and attempt to preserve it, while trauma induces resistance and recoil from what seems to have already happened.

In both cases, the same underlying movements are at work—grasping and rejection. Time merely shifts their direction. Whether the mind is holding onto a pleasant memory, avoiding a painful one, chasing a hoped-for outcome, or bracing against a feared possibility, it is engaging in the same pattern of control.

Seen this way, attachment is not limited to liking or disliking; both attraction and aversion bind attention. The symmetry reveals that much of our inner life is shaped less by what we experience, and more by how tightly we try to hold on or push away—past or future alike.
