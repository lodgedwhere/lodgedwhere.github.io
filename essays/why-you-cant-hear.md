**Why you can't hear the truth**

A symbolic mind learns by expanding and refining a network of distinctions. New information is incorporated only if it can be related to existing nodes in a way that preserves overall coherence. The system’s priority is not truth, but self‑consistency.

A pointer that reveals the provisionality of the entire network cannot be integrated, because integration would require the system to invalidate the very distinctions it uses to interpret the pointer. In other words:

A message that exposes the groundlessness of the system cannot be absorbed by the system without contradiction.

The mind can add content, rearrange content, or prune content — but it cannot assimilate a statement that undermines the rules by which content becomes meaningful.

Thus, insights that reveal the ungrounded nature of distinction are not “learned.”

They are seen only when the authority of the learning structure itself relaxes.
