**Now is transcendent**

The present moment is transcendent precisely because it escapes all frameworks of comparison or evaluation. Any attempt to measure it—whether by past experience, future expectation, or conceptual criteria—already slices it into fragments, imposing artificial limits.

Its infinity lies in this simultaneity: it contains all possible meaning and yet is meaningless if approached as a concept. Meaning arises only as an expression within it, not as an attribute of it. In other words, the present moment is the ground from which both understanding and misunderstanding emerge—and that very impossibility of fully grasping it is what makes it sacred.

You could even say it’s a form of Love — the epic paradox of Birth and Death united — manifesting as awareness: unlimited, nonjudgmental, and utterly immediate. It cannot be “had” or “understood”—only lived.
