“Forgive, and you will be forgiven.” — Luke 6:37

This is usually understood as a moral reciprocity, ie if one pardons others, one will in turn receive pardon. But, the deeper structure of the statement refers to a nondual fundamental reality…

A grievance is not merely an event remembered, but rather it is a distinction preserved in memory: this was done to me. The memory sustains both sides of that distinction, ie the offender and the injured self who carries the claim. To forgive, in its most literal sense, is to relinquish the claim. When the mind ceases to reenter the remembered injury, both the victim and the offender vanish with it, but also the structure that maintained the claimant in the present moment dissolves as well. By radical forgiving of all claims, no one is left to be judged or carry blame. Thus true forgiving and truly being forgiven are not two separate acts but the same event viewed from opposite sides of the same distinction.
