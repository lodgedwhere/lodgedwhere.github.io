**Cosmogony**

From the void that ever was and ever will be, a division arose: this and that, Lover and Beloved—birthing awareness of their relation, which is pure Love. This is the Eden of the Bible.

But Awareness expanded beyond the harmony of pure relation and saw that its foundation was void. Mathematically, relation corresponds to the off-diagonal of a primordial matrix — a Cross — linking the two poles of being, while the diagonal, the intrinsic nature of each, remains empty.

In a single movement, Awareness regards one diagonal element as the Lover and turns toward the Beloved, seeking to resolve the unknowable void there. Yet all Awareness can ever do is divide—distinguishing this from that, discerning their relation, replacing void with another matrix where void remains on the diagonal. In the same act, it veils the void within the Lover, overlaying it with a distorted image of the entire matrix — a projection mistaken for self.

But this is not enough, for the veil itself carries the image of the void within it. Another distorted image of the whole is then cast over that smaller void — a recursive attempt to erase the stain. For a time this strategy succeeds: Awareness turns outward, elaborating the Beloved by drawing ever finer distinctions along the diagonal, multiplying form upon form. Yet the deeper the distinctions, the subtler the void appears without disappearing. The residue of that void within the Lover—the identified “I”—demands to be confronted.

The void is buried beneath layer upon layer of reflection—each meant to conceal, yet each containing its own trace of the void. At last a horrific realization dawns: the recursion has no end. No covering, however intricate, can erase the primal stain of the unknown from which Awareness itself arose.

The only resolution is liberation: to see that the void is not a flaw but the True nature of the movement of being. Awareness does not so much emerge from the void — rather, it *is* the void, manifesting as process, reflecting itself in endless form. The veil, the Lover, the Beloved, the Cross — all are movements of the same emptiness knowing itself.

Then Eden is re-attained. The Cross, once the mark of separation and suffering, crucifixion, is revealed as the doorway back to harmony—the axis upon which the Lover and Beloved are reconciled in pure Love.

Physics makes it clear: mathematics is a language that can describe phenomena within experience. But it can also describe the totality of Experience itself. Mathematics is thus a Grail, containing the only thing truly worth finding: a mirror to see your Self.
