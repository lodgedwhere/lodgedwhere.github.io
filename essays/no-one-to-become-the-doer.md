**No one to become the “doer”**

The ego is not an entity but the appearance of subjecthood arising from a defended interpretive stance; it is not something that has beliefs, but the stabilization of belief itself as a presumed center.

Belief, in this sense, is not a cause operating within the world but a condition that fixes what counts as appearance at all. Because causality applies only among appearances, belief cannot function as a causal agent without committing a category error.

It follows that neither the so-called “true self” nor the ego possesses agency: the former because it is not an object within appearance, and the latter because it is a stance mistaken for one.

Appearances unfold and change, but no agent performs them; nothing is “done” (as an act), even within illusion, and the sense of doing is itself part of what appears.
