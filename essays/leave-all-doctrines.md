**“… to leave all doctrines and all teachers and to reach my goal alone – or die.”**

Anyone who calls themselves a “teacher”
has, at best, only grasped the shock of encountering the Absolute
and then wrapped that shock in concepts, language, and identity.

They have witnessed the boundary dissolve,
felt the ground fall away,
seen the “I” collapse back into the Whole —
and then stitched the experience back together
into a persona that can be spoken of, admired, followed.

But intellectualizing the wound is not the same as living from the silence that opened it.

The trauma of recognizing the truth
can be narrated, analyzed, systematized —
yet the living expression of that truth
requires that no one remain to claim it.

To live as the truth is to stand without position,
without role,
without the subtle flattery of being the one who “knows.”

The moment someone says,
“I am the teacher,”
a knot has already formed —
a new center of gravity has already reconstituted itself
inside the very freedom they glimpsed.

At best, they remember the blast
and speak from its echo.
At worst, they mistake the echo for the source.

The real thing leaves no one behind to teach.

It shines through whoever has disappeared,
but it never says,
“This is me,”
or
“I did this,”
or
“Follow.”
