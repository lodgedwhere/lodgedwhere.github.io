**Pulling on your bootstraps to stop sinking**

The present moment does not require interpretation, support, justification, or stabilization by thought. It *is*, independent of mind.

Any attempt to ground experience, to “understand” it, can only introduce *internal* constraints, ie conceptual structures that are inherently relational. To constrain is to distinguish, and distinction generates a polarized duality from what is fundamentally whole. What appears as an effort to secure reality in “meaning” is therefore already a movement away from the undivided immediacy it seeks to protect.

The mind’s crystallization of thought is then truly analogous to pulling on your bootstraps to stop sinking.

Language exemplifies this closed structure. Names acquire meaning only through differences and relations to other names, forming a self-referential network of distinctions. When this relational system attempts to capture what is *prior* to relation (immediate Being itself) it collapses into tautology or paradox.

Statements like “I am that I am” do not add information because nothing stands outside the whole to provide contrast. Representation always appears within what it represents; the present does not depend on description, while description depends entirely on the present.

Even the seemingly fundamental polarity of past and future follows the same logic. Past and future are never encountered except as present appearances: memories, anticipations, mental images. The felt need to stabilize the moment by reference to what was or what may be is thus another internally generated tension. The attempt to “stabilize the present” with “meaning” presumes that what is occurring might fail to be what it is…. but this is a possibility that only exists as thought!

Seen clearly, the present moment is *self-grounding* because it is the condition for anything to appear at all, including doubt, meaning, or the sense of self. The search for an external ground relaxes not by acquiring a new belief, but by recognizing the futility of imposing internal dualities upon what is already complete. In other words, absolute truth exists only in the *absence* of belief.
